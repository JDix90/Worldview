# Phase 1 plan — "the panel earns its rank"
**Written 2026-07-22 · derived from [the experience analysis](../reviews/2026-07-22-experience-analysis.md) · Phase 0 complete (DECISIONS #116–118)**

---

## 1. The constraint that shapes everything

The Go/No-Go gate is **~2026-07-30**. The briefing streak stands at **3 days** (07-20, 07-21, 07-22) and needs seven clean consecutive days, which lands on **07-26** — before the gate, with no slack for another failure.

Two distinct risks govern what may ship, and they are not the same axis:

| Risk | What it threatens | Browser work | Pi / worker work |
|---|---|---|---|
| **(a) Technical** — a restart breaks the streak | the gate's evidence | none | real, small |
| **(b) Methodological** — changing the artifact under measurement | the gate's *validity* | **yes** | **yes** |

Risk (a) is the obvious one and it exonerates browser work. Risk (b) is the one worth taking seriously, and it does not.

FOUNDATION §11 asks *"was it opened voluntarily on days when nothing pushed?"* and *"did it surface something genuinely informative?"* Those are questions about a **stable artifact**. We have already reshaped both surfaces heavily this week — new IA, crime map, radar, section order, promoted briefing. If we keep going through 07-30, the gate measures our recent edits rather than ORRERY.

**This tightens what I said earlier today.** I initially proposed shipping the browser-only items now on the grounds that they carry no soak risk. That is true of risk (a) and irrelevant to risk (b) — and the Journal in particular is designed to make the briefing more re-readable, which is *precisely the behaviour Q2 measures*. Shipping it mid-window would contaminate the answer in the flattering direction, which is the worst kind.

### Recommendation — as ratified by the owner, 2026-07-22

> **Build Phase 1 now. Deploy nothing during the build.** When everything is
> built and verified, the owner reviews the finished work and decides then
> whether to deploy before or after the gate — the deploy decision is
> deferred to that review, not pre-committed either way.

Everything below is implementable and fully verifiable against a local preview (the dev proxy reads the Pi's API read-only). Work lands on the branch, unshipped. The only things that touch the running system before 07-30 are invisible to the experience (§4).

This is not idle time: it is roughly the full Phase 1 build, ready to deploy the day the gate closes — and gated on its outcome, since a NO-GO makes most of it moot.

## 2. Track A — browser (build now, deploy post-gate)

### A1. Journal — the diary becomes re-readable · effort S–M · should-have
**Problem.** `/api/briefings` accepts `limit` and the UI fetches exactly one. The shadow log has an endpoint and no page. Sixty days of signals sit behind a 50-item feed. The owner cannot answer *"what did it tell me last Tuesday?"* — and the briefing is the best artifact the system produces.

**Experience.** A third FEED view, `JOURNAL`: past briefings newest-first, date-listed, each expandable in place, with that day's S1/S2 counts inline. Below, a signal history filtered by day and detector. Deliberately **not** infinite scroll or full-text search — 60 days, newest first, done.

**Requirements.** Reuse `/api/briefings?limit=30` (exists). `/api/signals` needs a `day` or `before` param (~5 lines, read-only). Reuse the `MarkdownLite` renderer already in `FeedPanel.tsx` so briefings render with their bold/headers rather than raw asterisks.

**Edge cases.** Fewer than N briefings → render what exists, no skeleton rows. A day with a *missing* briefing must show as a gap, not be silently skipped — the 07-17/19 loss (#116) is exactly what a journal should make visible.

**Acceptance.** Owner can read any briefing from the last 30 days and see, per day, what the instrument said and what it saw.

### A2. Find-a-flight · effort S · should-have
**Problem.** For a flight instrument, *"where is UA1234 right now"* has no answer short of hunting dots.

**Experience.** `/` opens a small HUD search. Matches callsign or hex against the client-side store (plus the mil store); Enter flies the globe there via `pointOfView` and opens the aircraft card — both already exist. No server work; the hot store is already client-side.

**Edge cases.** No match → honest empty state naming the corpus searched (*"not among 12,596 aircraft currently tracked"*), never a bare "no results". Ambiguous prefix → top 5 with routes.

**Acceptance.** A callsign visible on the globe is reachable by typing it, in under two seconds.

## 3. Track B — Pi (build now, deploy post-gate, contingent on GO)

### B1. Attention-aware carousel · effort M · should-have
**Problem.** The rotation is strictly periodic: a Tornado Warning gets the same 6 s slot in the same position as a quiet HAZARDS page. Red-wake wakes the *screen* but not the *priority*.

**Experience.** Three rules over the existing dwell table:
- **Red promotion** — a page whose content is red (HAZARDS with severe/extreme, NOW red status, SYSTEM on battery) moves to position 1 at 1.5× dwell until it clears, with a thin red left-edge bar so the grammar is learnable (*first + barred = urgent*).
- **Contextual insertion** — a RADAR page exists **only** when precip is within ~40 mi, otherwise it is not in the rotation at all.
- **Time-of-day** — SKY joins only from 15:00 to dawn; its content is tonight's sky.

**Requirements.** Hysteresis of 2 consecutive polls before promoting *or* demoting, so a value hovering at a threshold cannot ping-pong. Footer dots must reflect the live rotation length. **Every rule wrapped in try/except that falls back to the static 9-page rotation** — the carousel must never die of cleverness.

**Dependencies.** The battery predicate is already shipped (#118). RADAR needs B3 below. Needs the fixture harness (§4).

**Acceptance.** With forced fixtures, mock renders show promotion, insertion and daytime shortening; during the next real NWS alert the page is on-screen within one cycle and first thereafter, confirmed by framebuffer readback.

### B2. Dusk palette · effort S · could-have
Software-dim the palette (~×0.7 luminance, cyan→teal warm shift) between sunset and the sleep window, using the sunset time already fetched. `bl_power` on this HAT is binary (confirmed), so hardware dimming is not available — this must be software. The CRIME basemap already taught the calibration (#115).

### B3. RainViewer radar · effort M · could-have
Verified during the analysis: keyless, `access-control-allow-origin: *`, 12 past frames plus forecast, tile host live. Reuse the tile→mosaic path built for the crime map on both surfaces. **Honest scoping:** GIBS IMERG stays the global drape (RainViewer's coverage is radar-network-limited); RainViewer is used only in home-local contexts, where it is the only ~10-minute option against IMERG's multi-hour lag.

### B4. SKY moon disc · effort S · could-have
Draw the phase disc beside the text — `sky.py` already computes illumination and waxing. Port the half-disc + terminator-ellipse construction from `dashViz.tsx`, whose sign convention was established the hard way (#111). Pure identity parity; nearly free.

## 4. Track C — safe to do before the gate (invisible to the experience)

These change no user-facing behaviour, so risk (b) does not apply:

1. **Mock fixture harness** — `--fixture red|battery|radar|quiet` on the pager's mock path, so B1's rules are verifiable off-device. Developer tooling; the pager renders identically off-device by design. **This is a prerequisite for B1 and should be built first.**
2. **Rotate the ntfy topic** — it grants read *and* post to anyone who learns it, and post-gate it carries S1 anomaly content rather than only power events. One `.env` value plus re-subscribing in the app; cheap now, annoying later. Requires a worker restart, so pair it with any other worker change or accept one deliberate restart.
3. **Nothing else.** Explicitly out of scope before 07-30: any change to page content, ordering, dwell, layer defaults, analyst prompts/models/schedules, or detector thresholds.

In that case Track B is discarded rather than deployed, and Track A is reconsidered on its own merits. **Nothing in this plan may be written to presume the gate passed** — that constraint is in the constitution, and it applies to plans as much as to code.

## 6. Verification

Per item, before any deploy:
- **Browser** — `pnpm --filter @orrery/web typecheck && build`; `preview_start` against the local dev server (which proxies the Pi's API read-only); screenshot each state including empty and error states.
- **Pi** — render every page to PNG off-device via the pager's `--mock --shot-all` path at **both** `480x320` and `240x280`. This is where B1's rules and B2's palette get tuned. Rendering off-device found *two* independent causes of the briefing truncation when fixing either alone would have looked like a fix (#118) — that lesson is why this step is mandatory, not optional.
- **Post-gate deploy** — browser bundle first (zero device risk), then Pi items in a single `orrery-display` restart, then framebuffer readback to confirm. Worker untouched unless C2 is bundled in.
